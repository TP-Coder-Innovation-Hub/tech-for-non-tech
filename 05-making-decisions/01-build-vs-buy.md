# Build vs Buy
## The Decision Framework

Every time your organization needs a new capability, you face the same choice: build it yourself, buy an existing solution, or hire someone to handle it.

This is not a technical decision. It is a business decision with four dimensions:

> 🖼️ **[IMAGE_PLACEHOLDER]** — build vs buy vs hire decision framework uniqueness cost time control

| Dimension | Build | Buy (SaaS / Vendor) | Hire (Agency / Contractor) |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Uniqueness** | High (custom to your needs) | Low (standardized for everyone) | Medium (customized, built by others) |
| **Upfront cost** | High (engineering time) | Low (monthly fee) | Medium (project fee) |
| **Time to launch** | Long (months) | Fast (days/weeks) | Medium (weeks/months) |
| **Ongoing cost** | Low-Medium (your team maintains) | Recurring (subscription) | Varies (depends on contract) |
| **Control** | Full control | Limited to vendor's features | Shared control |
| **Maintenance burden** | All on you | Vendor handles it | Depends on agreement |

## When to Build

**Your need is genuinely unique.** No existing product does what you need because your process is different from everyone else's. If your competitive advantage depends on this capability, building may be the only option.

**You need full control.** You cannot tolerate vendor limitations, downtime, or changes. Your core product requires it.

**You have the team.** Building requires skilled engineers who are available and interested. If you need to hire a team from scratch to build one feature, the calculus shifts.

## When to Buy

**The capability is not your differentiator.** Email delivery, payment processing, file storage, customer support tools -- these are solved problems. Pay for the solution and spend your engineering talent on what makes you unique.

**Speed matters.** You need it next month, not next year. A SaaS product is available today. A build project takes months.

**You want to avoid maintenance.** Every system you build, you maintain. Every system you buy, the vendor maintains.

## When to Hire

**You need expertise you do not have.** The agency has built this type of system before. Your team has not.

**It is a one-time project.** A website redesign, a data migration, an initial system setup. Not ongoing work that requires a permanent team.

**You want to validate before committing.** Hire an agency for a prototype, then decide whether to build a permanent team.

## Hidden Costs to Consider

| Cost Category | Often Overlooked |
|---|---|
| **Migration** | Moving from one solution to another later is expensive |
| **Training** | Your team needs to learn whatever you choose |
| **Integration** | How does it connect to your existing systems? |
| **Opportunity cost** | What could your team have built instead? |
| **Vendor lock-in** | How hard is it to switch away later? |
| **Internal maintenance** | Custom-built software requires ongoing engineering attention |

## Why This Matters for You

Most organizations default to building (engineers like building) or buying (managers like speed). The right answer depends on the specific situation.

For each capability, ask: "Is this core to our competitive advantage? How fast do we need it? What does it cost over 3 years, not just 1? What happens if we need to change course?"

Build what differentiates you. Buy what does not. Hire for expertise gaps. Simple in theory, difficult in practice -- because it requires honest assessment of what actually makes your business unique.
